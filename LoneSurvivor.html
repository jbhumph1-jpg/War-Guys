Operation Redwing

Marcus Luttrell

Operation Redwing was meant to bring in a known terrorist and was conducted with a four man ground team that Marcuss Luttrell was a part of. The mission had been cancelled several different times since they had lost track of the target. The target they were after was constantly moving around so that he wouldn't get caught. The ground team touched down from helos when it was pouring rain and cold in the mountains in afghanistan. They had to make their way throught he mountains while it was pouring rain to a village where the target was believed to be and then either Luttrell or "Axe" would take the shot. As they were going through the mountains they slipped and fell a lot and Marcus was the slowest of the four as he was the tallest and had the most bulk. They planned to wait in the mountains for up to four days to take out the target. Some farmers stumbled on where they were laying out in the mountains and the had to let them go because of the stupid ROE(Rules of Engagement). Basically what happened next was the farmers went to the local village where the taliban target was with his 80-200 troops and they went after the SEALs. Axe, Mikey, and Dan died after they let the shepherds go. Marcus was wounded badly and ended up in a local pashtun village which protected him from the Taliban soldiers(not really soldiers i just couldn't think of a better term) sent to finish him off. He was eventually rescued by a group of Army Rangers.

Books

He wrote several different books, his most famous book is Lone Survivor which details the events of operation Redwing and it also provides a great look into BUD/S. This book is one of the best ones I've read without a doubt, it doesn't just show the men as cogs in the machine but it actually shows personallity and humor. He has written a few other books that I haven't read but probably will.

Life

He grew up in Texas. As a kid he was very active and went through some hard times as a kid. When the Saudis flooded the US oil market in the 80's it made his family businnes of breeding and selling horses. After this his family lost most of the land they had since the horses they bought were being sold at less than paid for. When he was about 15 he asked a local ex-Green Beret to train him for BUD/S. For his training Billy Shelton would have them run up to 12 miles with concrete blocks and had them workout at a local gym all the time and he always watched them and never let them be idle while training. He was good friends with Chris Kyle as mentioned several times in American Sniper and later he talked about Kyle after his death in 2013.
